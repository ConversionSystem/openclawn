## Executive Summary

This research focuses on finding the **ideal short, memorable name** for your AI Assistant. Based on naming psychology, competitor analysis, and trademark considerations, we've identified the best options across multiple categories.

### Key Naming Principles Applied
1. **Brevity** - 2-4 letters, 1-2 syllables (optimal for recall)
2. **Memorability** - Easy to spell, pronounce, and remember
3. **Uniqueness** - Low trademark conflict risk
4. **Global Appeal** - Works across languages and cultures
5. **Emotional Resonance** - Triggers positive associations

## Naming Psychology Quick Facts

### Why Short Names Win
- **Memory**: 7¬±2 chunks (Miller's Law) - shorter = easier recall
- **Word-of-Mouth**: Easy to say = more sharing
- **Branding**: Less visual clutter in logos
- **Global**: Fewer pronunciation issues

### Optimal Characteristics
- 1-2 syllables (maximum 3)
- 3-6 letters (sweet spot: 4)
- Consonant + vowel patterns (easier pronunciation)
- Unique opening sound (stands out)

### Sound Symbolism
| Sound | Association |
|-------|-------------|
| K, T, P | Strong, decisive |
| L, M, N | Soft, friendly |
| S, Z | Smooth, modern |
| V, F | Sophisticated |
| R | Powerful, rolling |

**KYRA**: K (strong) + Y (unique) + R (powerful) + A (open) = **Excellent balance**
